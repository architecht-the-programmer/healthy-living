Why Sleep..?

In this busy world we're living in,sleeping is now being viewed as a luxury rather than a necessity.This is causing people to live in 'debt'.This sleep debt develops when one does not get enough sleep either by voluntary sleep deprivation or by involuntary deprivation which may be caused by illness.The demands and distractions of modern life have whittled away people's sleeping time.Researchers estimate that earth's population is now getting,on average,an hour less of sleep per night than what is needed.However, getting enough sleep should not be optional and this page shows why!


The importance of getting enough sleep

Experts say that getting enough sleep helps in:

  • - Growth and development in children and teenagers
  • - Learning and retention of new information
  • - Maintaining the right balance of hormones that impact metabolism and weight
  • - Cadiovascular health
  • - Disease prevention
Tip!!

Rather than depriving yourself of sleep,you should simply get some rest.This is especially important when you are are sick.


Effects of sleep deprivation

Short-term effects

  • - Drowsiness
  • - Sudden mood swings
  • - Loss of capacity to create,plan and carry out activities
  • - Loss of concentration

Long-term effects

  • - Obesity
  • - Premature aging
  • - Fatigue
  • - Increased risk of infections,diabetes and cardiovascular diseases
  • - Chronic memory loss
  • - Depression

What if you're having problems getting enough sleep?
  • - Exercise at the right time of the day.For example in the morning or afternoon.
  • - Try to go to bed and get up at the same time each day.
  • - Make your bedroom quiet,dark and relaxing.
  • - Do not watch TV or use gadgets while in bed.
  • - Make your sleeping place as comfortable as possible.
  • - Avoid heavy meals,caffeine and alcohol before bedtime.

***Reversing sleep debt is a complex challenge.But understanding the importance of a healthy sleeping cycle can provide the motivation to change.***


Note: If these tips do not work for you,consult a qualified health-care proffesional.
